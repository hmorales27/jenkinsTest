Frequently Asked Questions

Accessing Journal Content

Q. How can I access the full text journal content from the app?

A. There are two ways you can gain access:

  1. If you are currently a subscriber to the journal, you can log in using the same username/email and password combination you use to access the journal content on the journal's web site.
  2. You can purchase issues or an annual subscription through the Apple iTunes Store.

Q. I’m a subscriber, what do I need in order to access the full features?

A. You can log in using the same username/email and password combination you use to access the journal content on the journal's web site.

If you don’t have a username/email and password, you can get one by first going to the Journal web site to register for an account and claiming access to your subscription . This one-time process only takes a few minutes. After you have an account, you can login in this app to gain access to the journal content.

Q. Can I access the app using my proxy server or TPS login?

A. These types of access are not currently supported. If you currently access the journal web site via TPS, you can register for an account on the journal web site and then use this new account to gain full text access in this app.

Notifications

Q. Can I receive notifications on my device when new issues are available?

A. Yes, if you can turn on notifications within the app by tapping the "Setting" icon (iPad) or going to the "Settings" menu (iPhone) and then to "Push Notifications".

You can also choose to have the app automatically download issues as soon as they are available. This feature called "Automatic Downloads" can be turned on by tapping the "Settings" icon (iPad) or going to the "Settings" menu (iPhone) and then to "Push Notifications".

Past Issues

Q. What past issues are available?

A. Past issues are available back through the start of 2011 in the app for both current journal subscribers as well as annual subscriptions purchased through the iTunes Store.

Downloading Issues

Q. How can I download journal issues?

A. Full text of articles are downloaded for each full issue when you ask to do so by tapping the Download Issue button. Until then, only the tables of contents and/or abstracts are available. You can continue to work while issues download in the background, reading articles that have been downloaded to that point.

You can also choose to have the app automatically download issues as soon as they are available. This feature called "Automatic Downloads" can be turned on by tapping the "Settings" icon (iPad) or going to the "Settings" menu (iPhone) and then to "Push Notifications".

Q. How can I find out how much of an issue remains to be downloaded?

A. The download icon will show a number over it when articles are downloading. Tap the download icon (iPad) or Download section (iPhone menu) at any time to check the progress of the download.

Deleting Content & App Memory Usage

Q. Can I delete content that I download to the app?

A. You can delete issues, articles, and/or multimedia from this app to reduce the amount of space used by the app's journal content on your device. Tap the "Setting" icon (iPad) or go to the "Settings" menu (iPhone) and then to "Usage" to delete downloaded content. Articles in your reading list and those with notes will not be deleted.

Articles in Press/Online First

These are articles that have been approved for publication but are not yet published in an issue.

Q. Are these articles included with iTunes Subscriptions?

A. Yes, access to the full text of these articles is included with annual subscriptions purchased through the iTunes Store. Access to these articles is not provided with issue-only purchases through the iTunes Store.

Q. How can I download Articles in Press/Online First articles?

A. You can download them in a single batch by tapping Download All or individually by tapping the article or the download button to its right.

Offline Reading

Q. How can I read article offline?

A. 1. You can download issues and Articles in Press/Online First articles to read. Once downloaded, these will be available to read offline.

2. Articles saved to your reading list are always available offline. They will not be deleted when issues are deleted.

3. To read PDFs offline, each must be downloaded. Once downloaded, it will be available to read offline.

Q. How can I add articles to my reading list to read offline?

A. Tap the star icon (iPad) or the "Add to Reading List" button (iPhone) to add an article to your reading list.

Q. Are articles in my reading list also saved to the journal web site or to my other devices?

A. No, this is not currently available. Articles in your reading list are only available in the app on this device.

Notes

Q. How can I use notes within the app?

A. Notes are your own personal comments that you attach to articles. To add notes, drag to select text in the full text of an article. You will be prompted to enter your note. Their presence is indicated by appearance with a yellow highlight on the text you selected to add the note.

Q. Can I get my notes out of the app?

A. Yes, you can email your notes from the app. Go to the "Notes" section within the app or within an article and tap on the mail icon to email your notes.

Q. Will my notes be deleted when issues are deleted?

A. No, your notes and the articles they are associated with will remain in the app. You can find your notes under the "Notes" section of the app.

Q. Are my notes also saved to the journal web site or to my other devices?

A. No, this is not currently available. Notes are only available in the app on this device.
